# Nakshatra Corpus — Characteristics of the 27 Birth Stars

Each nakshatra spans 13°20' of the sidereal zodiac. The Moon's position at birth determines the janma nakshatra (birth star).

---

## 1. Ashwini (0°–13°20' Aries)
**Lord:** Ketu | **Deity:** Ashwin Kumars (divine physicians) | **Symbol:** Horse's head

Ashwini natives are energetic, quick, and independent. They are natural healers and pioneers who act with speed and decisiveness. They possess a youthful, enthusiastic nature and strong leadership qualities. They can be impulsive and restless. Key traits: courage, initiative, healing ability, love of travel. They dislike being controlled and thrive when given freedom. They are generally healthy but prone to head-related ailments.

---

## 2. Bharani (13°20'–26°40' Aries)
**Lord:** Venus | **Deity:** Yama (god of death and dharma) | **Symbol:** Yoni (womb)

Bharani natives are creative, passionate, and carry a strong sense of responsibility. They deal with themes of birth, death, and transformation. They are loyal, hard-working, and deeply devoted. They can be stubborn and unwilling to accept failure. Key traits: perseverance, creativity, sensuality, justice. They excel in careers related to medicine, legal work, arts, and leadership. They hold strong moral values.

---

## 3. Krittika (26°40' Aries–10° Taurus)
**Lord:** Sun | **Deity:** Agni (fire god) | **Symbol:** Razor or flame

Krittika natives are sharp, direct, and courageous. They have a burning drive to achieve and are capable of cutting through obstacles. They are deeply principled and honest but can be sharp-tongued. Key traits: determination, willpower, leadership, critical thinking. They are protective of loved ones. They may have a tendency toward pride. They excel in the military, fire-related professions, cooking, and public administration.

---

## 4. Rohini (10°–23°20' Taurus)
**Lord:** Moon | **Deity:** Brahma (creator) | **Symbol:** Ox cart or chariot

Rohini is considered the most fertile and beautiful nakshatra, the Moon's favourite abode. Natives are charming, artistic, and fond of beauty, luxury, and material comforts. They are romantic, sensual, and emotionally rich. Key traits: creativity, charisma, love of nature, good taste. They have a magnetic personality and excellent communication skills. They may become possessive or overly materialistic. They thrive in arts, music, business, and hospitality.

---

## 5. Mrigashira (23°20' Taurus–6°40' Gemini)
**Lord:** Mars | **Deity:** Soma (Moon god) | **Symbol:** Deer's head

Mrigashira natives are curious, restless, and always searching for something. They are gentle yet lively, highly inquisitive, and love to travel. They have a poetic and artistic nature. Key traits: curiosity, research ability, wit, adaptability. They can be indecisive and scattered. They make excellent writers, researchers, travellers, and artists. They are drawn to new experiences and dislike monotony.

---

## 6. Ardra (6°40'–20° Gemini)
**Lord:** Rahu | **Deity:** Rudra (storm god, fierce form of Shiva) | **Symbol:** Teardrop or diamond

Ardra natives are intense, deeply emotional, and have the capacity for great transformation. They undergo storms in life that ultimately purify them. They are intelligent, analytical, and persistent. Key traits: depth, research ability, emotional intensity, transformation. They can be stubborn and destructive when provoked. They excel in science, research, engineering, and medicine. Their life often involves significant upheavals that lead to renewal.

---

## 7. Punarvasu (20° Gemini–3°20' Cancer)
**Lord:** Jupiter | **Deity:** Aditi (goddess of boundlessness) | **Symbol:** Quiver of arrows

Punarvasu natives are optimistic, generous, and spiritually inclined. They have a gift for renewal and recovery. They are kind, forgiving, and philosophical. Key traits: wisdom, generosity, renewal, contentment. They are adaptable and can recover from setbacks with grace. They may spread themselves too thin. They excel in teaching, philosophy, spirituality, and counselling.

---

## 8. Pushya (3°20'–16°40' Cancer)
**Lord:** Saturn | **Deity:** Brihaspati (Jupiter, divine teacher) | **Symbol:** Flower, circle, or arrow

Pushya is considered one of the most auspicious nakshatras. Natives are nurturing, wise, and deeply caring. They are generous, devoted, and responsible. Key traits: compassion, discipline, reliability, nourishment. They have strong family values and are loyal friends. They can be overly self-sacrificing. They excel in education, medicine, social work, and spiritual practice.

---

## 9. Ashlesha (16°40'–30° Cancer)
**Lord:** Mercury | **Deity:** Naga (serpent deities) | **Symbol:** Serpent coil

Ashlesha natives are perceptive, secretive, and psychically sensitive. They have a penetrating intelligence and sharp instincts. They can be manipulative but are also deeply wise. Key traits: cunning, intuition, healing power, mysticism. They are drawn to occult sciences and medicine. They can be possessive and hypnotic in relationships. They excel in research, psychology, and the healing arts.

---

## 10. Magha (0°–13°20' Leo)
**Lord:** Ketu | **Deity:** Pitris (ancestral spirits) | **Symbol:** Throne or palanquin

Magha natives have regal bearing and strong ambition. They are proud, authoritative, and connected to lineage and tradition. They have natural leadership qualities. Key traits: authority, pride, respect for elders and tradition, ambition. They can be arrogant. They thrive in positions of power, politics, and administration. They have a deep sense of honour and are loyal to family heritage.

---

## 11. Purva Phalguni (13°20'–26°40' Leo)
**Lord:** Venus | **Deity:** Bhaga (god of delight and marital happiness) | **Symbol:** Front legs of a cot or hammock

Purva Phalguni natives love luxury, beauty, and rest. They are charming, sensual, and artistically gifted. They enjoy the finer things in life and are often magnetic and attractive. Key traits: creativity, sensuality, generosity, love of pleasure. They can be lazy or self-indulgent. They excel in entertainment, arts, hospitality, and any creative field.

---

## 12. Uttara Phalguni (26°40' Leo–10° Virgo)
**Lord:** Sun | **Deity:** Aryaman (god of contracts and favours) | **Symbol:** Back legs of a cot, fig tree

Uttara Phalguni natives are helpful, organized, and socially conscious. They work hard for the benefit of others and believe in fair dealings. They are reliable, intellectual, and humanitarian. Key traits: service, reliability, analytical mind, social responsibility. They can be overly critical. They excel in social work, administration, education, and public service.

---

## 13. Hasta (10°–23°20' Virgo)
**Lord:** Moon | **Deity:** Savitur (form of Sun, stimulator of intelligence) | **Symbol:** Open hand or fist

Hasta natives are skillful, clever, and gifted with their hands. They are quick-witted, resourceful, and humorous. They have the ability to grasp things rapidly. Key traits: dexterity, intelligence, resourcefulness, humour. They can be manipulative or cunning. They excel in crafts, surgery, massage, writing, and business. They are adaptable and charming in social settings.

---

## 14. Chitra (23°20' Virgo–6°40' Libra)
**Lord:** Mars | **Deity:** Vishwakarma (divine architect) | **Symbol:** Bright jewel or pearl

Chitra natives are creative, artistic, and drawn to beauty and design. They have a strong aesthetic sense and love of architecture, fashion, and art. They are charismatic and attractive personalities. Key traits: creativity, aesthetic intelligence, charm, independence. They can be self-absorbed. They excel in architecture, design, jewellery, fine arts, and fashion.

---

## 15. Swati (6°40'–20° Libra)
**Lord:** Rahu | **Deity:** Vayu (wind god) | **Symbol:** Young plant shoot blowing in wind, sword

Swati natives are independent, diplomatic, and freedom-loving. They are flexible and adaptable, bending like the wind without breaking. They are skilled in business and diplomacy. Key traits: independence, diplomacy, flexibility, business acumen. They can be indecisive. They excel in trade, business, diplomacy, and teaching. They have a charming and agreeable nature.

---

## 16. Vishakha (20° Libra–3°20' Scorpio)
**Lord:** Jupiter | **Deity:** Indra and Agni (king of gods and fire god) | **Symbol:** Triumphal arch, potter's wheel

Vishakha natives are goal-oriented, determined, and competitive. They are persistent and focused on achieving their aims even if it takes a long time. They can be intense and demanding. Key traits: ambition, determination, single-mindedness, passion. They may be jealous or aggressive. They excel in leadership, politics, religion, and activism. Their life often builds toward a major achievement in the second half.

---

## 17. Anuradha (3°20'–16°40' Scorpio)
**Lord:** Saturn | **Deity:** Mitra (god of friendship and devotion) | **Symbol:** Lotus flower, staff

Anuradha natives are devoted, disciplined, and excellent at maintaining friendships and alliances. They can succeed in foreign lands. They are deeply loyal and emotionally intense. Key traits: devotion, friendship, discipline, occult interest. They can be possessive. They excel in organisations, spirituality, occult science, and international work. They find success away from birthplace.

---

## 18. Jyeshtha (16°40'–30° Scorpio)
**Lord:** Mercury | **Deity:** Indra (chief of gods) | **Symbol:** Circular amulet, umbrella, earring

Jyeshtha natives are powerful, responsible, and carry a natural sense of authority. They are often the eldest or the one who bears the most responsibility in the family. They are protective and strong. Key traits: authority, protection, wisdom, courage. They can be arrogant or overly self-important. They excel in administration, military, and leadership roles. They have a sharp intellect and hidden depths.

---

## 19. Mula (0°–13°20' Sagittarius)
**Lord:** Ketu | **Deity:** Nirrti (goddess of destruction and dissolution) | **Symbol:** Roots tied together, lion's tail

Mula natives are investigative, philosophical, and drawn to root causes. They have a strong desire to understand the foundations of things. They can experience upheaval and transformation. Key traits: research, investigation, spirituality, destructive creativity. They may face instability in early life. They excel in research, medicine, philosophy, and spiritual practice. Their path often involves uprooting old structures to find deeper truth.

---

## 20. Purva Ashadha (13°20'–26°40' Sagittarius)
**Lord:** Venus | **Deity:** Apas (goddess of water) | **Symbol:** Elephant tusk, fan, winnowing basket

Purva Ashadha natives are invincible, courageous, and highly idealistic. They are persuasive and inspiring speakers. They believe strongly in their convictions and defend them fiercely. Key traits: pride, boldness, philosophical nature, perseverance. They can be stubborn and unwilling to change their views. They excel in law, philosophy, writing, and leadership. They are purifying in nature, clearing away obstacles.

---

## 21. Uttara Ashadha (26°40' Sagittarius–10° Capricorn)
**Lord:** Sun | **Deity:** Visvedevas (universal gods) | **Symbol:** Elephant tusk, small bed

Uttara Ashadha natives are stable, responsible, and deeply principled. They achieve victory through patience and consistent effort. They are serious, idealistic, and universally minded. Key traits: integrity, perseverance, leadership, universal perspective. They can be serious to the point of being rigid. They excel in public service, research, writing, and positions of authority. Their victories are lasting.

---

## 22. Shravana (10°–23°20' Capricorn)
**Lord:** Moon | **Deity:** Vishnu (preserver of the universe) | **Symbol:** Ear or three footprints

Shravana natives are excellent listeners, learners, and connectors of knowledge. They are wise, generous, and spiritually inclined. They have a talent for learning and preserving knowledge. Key traits: listening ability, wisdom, generosity, spiritual seeking. They can be overly trusting. They excel in education, media, counselling, and spiritual work. The three footprints symbolize the three strides of Vishnu — they are far-reaching in their goals.

---

## 23. Dhanishtha (23°20' Capricorn–6°40' Aquarius)
**Lord:** Mars | **Deity:** Eight Vasus (deities of earthly abundance and prosperity) | **Symbol:** Drum (mridangam or tabla) or flute

Dhanishtha means "most famous" or "most swift" (also called Shravishtha). Natives born under Dhanishtha are ambitious, energetic, and musically gifted. They have a natural talent for rhythm, music, and the performing arts.

**Key characteristics:**
- Strong attraction to music, dance, and performing arts — many accomplished musicians and performers are born under this star
- Wealth-oriented and ambitious; the Eight Vasus bless them with material abundance and earthly prosperity
- Courageous, dynamic, and action-driven (lord is Mars)
- Generous and liberal — they enjoy sharing their wealth and resources with others
- Goal-oriented and determined, with the ability to lead and organise
- Good administrative ability; they can manage large projects and people
- Strong physical constitution, athletic ability, and love of sports
- Independent and self-reliant; they dislike being told what to do
- They may experience delays or complications in marriage due to the dual-sign nature (Capricorn/Aquarius) and Mars influence
- They have a broad, humanitarian outlook in the Aquarius portion, and a structured, disciplined nature in the Capricorn portion
- Potential weakness: pride, restlessness, and a tendency to be overly concerned with wealth and status
- They recover quickly from setbacks and have strong resilience

**Career:** Music, dance, performing arts, sports, military, engineering, management, finance, real estate.

**Friendly nakshatras:** Rohini, Hasta, Shravana. Neutral to most others.

---

## 24. Shatabhisha (6°40'–20° Aquarius)
**Lord:** Rahu | **Deity:** Varuna (god of cosmic waters, sky, and earth) | **Symbol:** Empty circle, 1,000 stars or flowers

Shatabhisha means "hundred physicians" or "hundred healers." Natives are secretive, independent, and deeply interested in healing, research, and mysticism. They have a unique, unconventional nature. Key traits: healing ability, mysticism, independent thinking, scientific aptitude. They can be reclusive or eccentric. They excel in medicine, astrology, research, and unconventional fields. They have strong intuition.

---

## 25. Purva Bhadrapada (20° Aquarius–3°20' Pisces)
**Lord:** Jupiter | **Deity:** Ajaikapada (one-footed god, fierce form of Shiva) | **Symbol:** Swords, two front legs of funeral cot, man with two faces

Purva Bhadrapada natives are idealistic, intense, and willing to sacrifice for a higher cause. They have passionate beliefs and can be transformative forces. Key traits: idealism, transformation, fiery passion, asceticism. They can be extreme in views. They excel in spirituality, philosophy, social reform, and unconventional careers. They often question society's norms and seek deeper meaning.

---

## 26. Uttara Bhadrapada (3°20'–16°40' Pisces)
**Lord:** Saturn | **Deity:** Ahirbudhnya (serpent of the deep, dragon of the cosmic ocean) | **Symbol:** Twins, back legs of a funeral cot, snake in water

Uttara Bhadrapada natives are deeply wise, patient, and spiritually profound. They have great depth of character and are excellent teachers and counsellors. Key traits: wisdom, patience, depth, compassion. They can be slow to act. They excel in teaching, writing, philosophy, and spiritual guidance. They carry karmic wisdom from many lifetimes.

---

## 27. Revati (16°40'–30° Pisces)
**Lord:** Mercury | **Deity:** Pushan (nourisher, protector of travellers and herds) | **Symbol:** Fish or a drum

Revati is the last nakshatra, completing the zodiacal cycle. Natives are compassionate, creative, and deeply spiritual. They are natural caretakers with a gentle and nurturing personality. Key traits: compassion, creativity, spiritual depth, protectiveness. They can be overly idealistic or escapist. They excel in the arts, healing, social work, and spiritual service. They carry an innate sense of completion and readiness for a new cycle.
